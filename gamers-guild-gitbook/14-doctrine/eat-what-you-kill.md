# Eat What You Kill

Eat What You Kill is a foundational doctrine of the GGUILD ecosystem.

It defines how value, reward, and responsibility should be understood.

## Core Principle

True value must be earned.

Not passively received.
Not assumed.
Not extracted without contribution.

## What It Means

You eat what you kill.

That means:

- reward follows contribution
- recognition follows work
- influence follows responsibility
- participation should produce visible value

## Why This Matters

Many digital systems reward:

- noise over effort
- speculation over contribution
- entitlement over discipline

GGUILD is built to resist that pattern.

## GGUILD Application

Inside GGUILD, this doctrine shapes:

- contributor rewards
- reputation systems
- governance trust
- community expectations
- ecosystem culture

## Cultural Standard

GGUILD is not designed for passive extraction.

It is designed for:

- builders
- creators
- contributors
- disciplined participants
- long-term thinkers

## Final Point

GGUILD is a system where value is meant to be earned, not simply claimed.
