# Eat What You Kill Doctrine

Eat What You Kill is a foundational philosophy informing the GGUILD ecosystem.

It defines how value, contribution, and reward should be understood.

---

## Core Idea

True value must be earned.

Not passively received.  
Not assumed.  
Not extracted without contribution.

---

## The Principle

You eat what you kill.

That means:

- you earn what you receive  
- you contribute before you benefit  
- you build before you expect reward  
- you create value before claiming value  

---

## Why This Matters

Modern systems often reward:

- attention over effort  
- noise over contribution  
- speculation over creation  

GGUILD is designed to move in the opposite direction.

---

## GGUILD Alignment

Inside GGUILD, this principle connects directly to:

- contributor rewards  
- reputation systems  
- governance participation  
- community recognition  
- ecosystem growth  

Contribution → Recognition → Reward

---

## The Cultural Standard

GGUILD is not built for passive participation.

It is built for:

- builders

- contributors

- competitors
