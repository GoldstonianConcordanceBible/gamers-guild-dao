# Risks and Disclosures

GGUILD is a real project idea with real ambition, but it also carries real risk.

This section exists to state that clearly and honestly.

## Core Principle

Documentation does not eliminate risk.

A roadmap does not guarantee execution.

A token does not guarantee adoption.

## Risk Categories

### Regulatory Risk

Digital asset regulation is evolving.

Different jurisdictions may interpret token activity differently, and future legal developments may affect how projects like GGUILD are treated.

### Market Risk

Public attention, liquidity conditions, and user behavior can shift quickly.

Token-related ecosystems may experience volatility, inconsistency, or low traction.

### Execution Risk

A project may fail to complete planned features, integrations, community systems, or governance structures.

Strong ideas do not automatically become strong systems.

### Technical Risk

Technical systems can fail.

This includes risks related to:

- smart contracts
- infrastructure
- APIs
- dashboards
- integrations
- automation systems

### Governance Risk

Governance can become weak if participation is low, unclear, captured by a few voices, or poorly documented.

### Community Risk

A project can have documentation and still fail to build a healthy community.

Without aligned participation, the system becomes weaker.

### Messaging Risk

Poor public messaging can create confusion, attract the wrong expectations, or weaken credibility.

This is especially important for token projects.

## Practical Disclosure

GGUILD should be understood as:

- an evolving project
- a structured experiment
- a community system in development
- a case study with uncertainty

## Final Point

The existence of risk does not invalidate the project.

But ignoring risk weakens trust.

Clear disclosure is part of serious project design.
